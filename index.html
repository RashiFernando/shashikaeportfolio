Project – Online Remittance Processing in Sri Lanka Economic Crisis

🔹 Project Overview

As a result of the Sri Lankan economic crisis in early 2023, Sri Lanka experienced a severe fuel crisis requiring most organization to work in remote conditions. It posed serious operational problems at Singer Sri Lanka PLC where I worked in the finance department especially in dealing with remittances and trade receivables.

🔹 Issue

The normal remittance process required submitting original payment documents by the sales promotion officers (SPOs). These were then confirmed and inputted into the IFS ERP system to update receivables and approve dealer orders within credit limits. With the shift to work-from-home:

  • There was no possibility of handing over physical documents.
  • A huge accumulation of unprocessed remittances built up.
  • Credit limits of dealers were not revised in time.
  • This slowed order approvals and affected the business.

🔹 My Role

This problem was identified as a high risk to the business continuity and receivable management as an Operations Finance Executive. I took initiative to:

  • Examine the issue and find gaps in the processes.
  • Discuss and conduct several Zoom meetings with supervisors and Area Sales Managers.
  • Liaise with top management and IT departments for immediate system development.
  • Introduce a management digital solution called “Online Remittance Processing System”.
  • Conduct training and implement within one week.

🔹 Solution

I suggested the introduction of a temporal online remittance processing system in place of the paperwork. We then discussed the idea with the Operations Finance Manager, after which we presented it to the Finance Director, and Operations Director and got their approval. The implementation included.

  • Working with the IT department to create a backend platform.
  • Enabling SPOs to post payment documents online.
  • Checking payments through the Bank Reconciliation System (BRS).
  • Creating a document reference number for each payment.
  • Entering certified information into the IFS system.

🔹 Implementation

After development, I organized Zoom training for over 100 sales officers and area managers. The process included:

  • Pilot testing prior to full implementation.
  • Switched to real-time data processing.

🔹 Outcome

  • The new system brought significant improvement.
  • Remittances backlog cleared successfully.
  • Ensured real-time updating of receivables.
  • Better dealer order approval process to support sales.
  • Reduced time lost on credit limit issues.
  • Removed reliance on hardcopy documents.
  • Time saved and lessened document storage needs (3000+ monthly slips).

🔹 Long-Term Impact

Even though it was implemented as a temporary measure during the crisis, the system was much more effective than the previous process. As a result:

  • Top management identified it as valuable beyond the crisis period and essential to streamline daily operations.
  • The IFS system later incorporated additional features.
  • It became a regular practice.
  • The project helped streamline organizational operations and enhance financial management.

🔹 Recognition

The current Financial Director and CEO officially acknowledged the effort made by me and my manager to implement this solution successfully and optimize the business processes in a difficult time.
